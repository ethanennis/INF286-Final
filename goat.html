Greatest Players of All Time




  1. LeBron James

    Accolades: 4x NBA Champion, 4x Finals MVP, 4x MVP, 17x All Star, 16x All NBA

    Bio: LeBron James is the only player in NBA history to lead 5 different head coaches to the NBA finals.

  2. Michael Jordan

    Accolades: 6x NBA Champion, 6x Finals MVP, 5x MVP, 14x All Star, 11x All NBA

    Bio: Michael Jordan is commonly refered to as the greatest basketball player of all time, and is credited with help globablize the sport of basketball as a member of the 1992 "Dream Team".

  3. Bill Russell

    Accolades: 11x NBA Champion, 5x MVP, 12x All Star, 11x All NBA

    Bio: Bill Russell holds the record for the most championships, and was the first black coach in american sports.

  4. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

    Accolades: 6x NBA Champion, 6x MVP, 19x All Star, 15x All NBA

    Bio: Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is commonly referred to as the greatest college basketball player and has an argument as the greatest professional basketball player of all time with a record 6 MVPs.

  5. Magic Johnson

    Accolades: 5x NBA Champion, 3x Finals MVP, 3x MVP, 12x All Star, 10x All NBA

    Bio: Earvin "Magic" Johnson is widely considered the greatest point guard in NBA history, and is also recognized for his work in the fight against HIV, helping spread awareness to millions.

  6. Wilt Chamberlain

    Accolades: 2x NBA Champion, 1972 Finals MVP, 13x All Star, 10x All NBA

    Bio: Wilt Chamberlain once averaged 50 points and 25 rebounds per game for an entire season, he also scored 100 points in one game that season.

  7. Tim Duncan

    Accolades: 5x NBA Champion, 3x Finals MVP, 2x MVP, 15x All Star, 15x All NBA

    Bio: Tim Duncan was born in the US Virgin Islands and grew up wanting to be a swimmer.

  8. Larry Bird

    Accolades: 3x NBA Champion, 2x Finals MVP, 3x MVP, 12x All Star, 10x All NBA

    Bio: Larry Bird is the only player in NBA history to win an MVP and the Coach of the Year award.

  9. Kobe Bryant

    Accolades: 5x NBA Champion, 2x Finals MVP, 2008 MVP, 18x All Star, 15x All NBA

    Bio: Kobe Bryant helped lead the Los Angeles Lakers to 5 NBA titles during the 2000s, Bryant also won an Oscar after playing.

  10. Shaquille O'Neal

    Accolades: 4x NBA Champion, 3x Finals MVP, 2000 MVP, 15x All Star, 14x All NBA

    Bio: Shaquille O'Neal has broken more backboards than made 3 pointers.

  11. Stephen Curry

    Accolades: 3x NBA Champion, 2x MVP, 7x All Star, 6x All NBA

    Bio: Wardell Stephen Curry is the most transcendent player of the 21st century, transforming the three point shot from a niche art, to a required skill, Curry also helped lead the greatest team of all time, the 2017 Golden State Warriors to a 16-1 playoff record.

  12. Kevin Durant

    Accolades: 2x NBA Champion, 2x Finals MVP, 2014 MVP, 11x All Star, 9x All NBA

    Bio: Kevin Durant is commonly considered the most complete scorer in NBA history.

  13. George Mikan

    Accolades: 5x BAA/NBA Champion, 4x All Star, 5x All BAA/NBA

    Bio: George Mikan led the Minneapolis Lakers to the NBA's first dynasty in the 1950s.

  14. Jerry West

    Accolades: 1972 NBA Champion, 1969 Finals MVP, 14x All Star, 12x All NBA

    Bio: Jerry West is the NBA's logo, and is known also as being arguably the greatest executive in NBA history, thanks to decisions such as, Drafting Magic Johnson or Kobe Bryant.

  15. . Julius Erving

    Accolades: 3x NBA/ABA Champion, 2x ABA Playoff MVP, 16x All Star, 12x All NBA/ABA

    Bio: Dr J. is known for his big afro and high flying dunks.

  16. John Havlicek

    Accolades: 8x NBA Champion, 1974 Finals MVP, 13x All Star, 11x All NBA

    Bio: John Havlicek was the side kick to Bill Russell, and in the 70s helped the Celtics continue their success, without their franchise's greatest player.

  17. Dwyane Wade

    Accolades: 3x NBA Champion, 2006 Finals MVP, 13x All Star, 8x All NBA

    Bio: Dwyane Wade led the Miami Heat to the 2006 NBA Championship with one of the greatest finals performances of all time against the Mavericks.

  18. Bob Pettit

    Accolades: 1958 NBA Champion, 2x MVP, 11x All Star, 11x All NBA

    Bio: Bob Pettit won the NBA's first MVP, and was a part of the only team to defeat Russell's Celtics in the NBA Finals.

  19. Dirk Nowitzki

    Accolades: 2011 NBA Champion, 2011 Finals MVP, 2007 MVP, 14x All Star, 12x All NBA

    Bio: Dirk Nowizki was the first German NBA All Star and helped lead Dallas to their first NBA Championship.

  20. Oscar Robertson

    Accolades: 1971 NBA Champion, 1964 MVP, 12x All Star, 11x All NBA

    Bio: Oscar Robertson is recognized as the first player in NBA history to average a triple double for an entire season, helping revolutionize the position. He also fought to introduce free agency: changing professional sports forever.

  21. Bob Cousy

    Accolades: 6x NBA Champion, 1957 MVP, 13x All Star, 12x All NBA

    Bio: Bob Cousy was one of the first NBA players to introduce a sort of flare and entertainment to the NBA, Cousy helped orcastrate the first half of the NBA's greatest dynasty: the 50s & 60s Celtics.

  22. Dolph Schayes

    Accolades: 1955 NBA Champion, 12x All Star, 12x All NBA

    Bio: Dolph Schayes led the Syracause Nationals to an NBA Championship and played his entire career there until his final year where the moved to Philidelphia where they became the 76ers.

  23. Kevin Garnett

    Accolades: 2008 NBA Champion, 2004 MVP, 15x All Star, 9x All NBA

    Bio: Kevin Garnett is one of five players to have won MVP and DPOY.

  24. Isiah Thomas

    Accolades: 2x NBA Champion, 1990 Finals MVP, 12x All Star, 5x All NBA

    Bio: Isiah Thomas led the Detroit "Bad Boy" Pistons to 3 straight NBA Finals and back to back championships.

  25. James Harden

    Accolades: 2018 MVP, 9x All Star, 7x All NBA

    Bio: James Harden led the Houston Rockets in 2018 only one win shy of defeating the Golden State Warriors, a team commonly considered unbeatable at the time.
